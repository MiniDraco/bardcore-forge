Circle of 5ths & color moves

Outer = major · inner = relative minor · ↻ each step = +1 ♯ (←♭)

Sharps: F C G D A E B · Flats: reverse. Adjacent keys = smoothest modulation. Relative = same notes (C↔Am) · Parallel = same root, swap mode (C↔Cm).

  • Borrowed iv in major — "saddest chord"
  • ♭VII / ♭VI / ♭III — rock & cinematic lift
  • Picardy 3rd — end minor on major = hope
  • Secondary dom V/V — extra forward pull
  • Tritone sub — chromatic jazz bass
  • Pedal point — drone under shifting chords

Meter & feel

Simpleby 22/4 3/4 4/4 — square

6/8 = 2 big beats of 3 (don't count all 6). Backbeat = snare on 2 & 4. Anacrusis = pickup before beat 1.

Swing

Straight 1:1 · light ~1.5:1 · hard shuffle 2:1 (triplet). Pocket = drums+bass locked. Behind = soulful, ahead = urgent.

Syncopation & cross-rhythm

  • Syncopation accent the "&" offbeats — funk/reggae/Latin
  • Anticipation hit just before the beat — pushes energy
  • Polyrhythm 3:2 3 against 2 ("nice-cup-of-tea")
  • Polymeter two meters at once, realign at LCM
  • Hemiola regroup 2×3/4 as 3×2/4

Dynamics & articulation

pp p mp mf f ff · cresc. < / dim. >. Staccato=short, legato=smooth, marcato=forceful. Subtract layers before a big hit.

Melody, Production & Genre Cheat-Codes

the tune, the texture, the recipes

Melody & the hook

  • Motif 2–7 note DNA cell, repeated & varied
  • Contour ascending=lift · descending=release · arch=satisfying · wave=conversational · static=hypnotic/rap
  • Conjunct stepwise = singable · Disjunct leaps = drama (one signature octave leap)
  • Tension tones 7/9/sus/blue notes = ache

Earworm = singable + distinctive rhythm + simple contour + tension→resolution + repetition-with-twist.

Lines: di·tri·tetra(4)·penta(5)·hexa·hepta·octameter. Most singable = tetra + tri alternating.

Named meters & variation

  • Iambic pentameter 5 iambs ("shall I com-PARE thee…")
  • Ballad / common meter 8.6.8.6, ABCB — half of all songs
  • Blank verse unrhymed iambic pentameter
  • Free verse no meter, no rhyme

Anti-robot variation

Trochaic inversion (flip line-start), spondaic brake, catalexis (drop final slack → end on stress), feminine ending (extra weak syllable = pickup note). Perfect meter = robotic; break it where the melody breathes.

Schemes: ABAB cross · AABB couplet · ABBA envelope · ABCB ballad · terza rima ABA-BCB.

Sound texture & flow

  • Alliteration initial consonants — grabby hooks
  • Assonance repeated vowels — inner glue
  • Consonance / sibilance repeated consonants / s-hiss
  • Plosives p·b·t·d·k·g — punch, anger
  • Liquids l·r·m·n — flowing, singable
  • Euphony / cacophony smooth vs harsh

Lineation

End-stopped sits inside the bar · enjambment spills across it (drive) · caesura = mid-line pause (the ad-lib slot).

Lyric, Flow & the Music↔Poetry Bridge

where it becomes a song — and a prompt

Prosody — fit word to music

  • Stress → strong beat never mis-stress ("gui-TAR," not "GUI-tar")
  • Open vowels on money notes "ah/oh" for peaks; "ee/ih" feel tight
  • Line = one breath can't sing it in a breath? cut it
  • Rhyme on the downbeat for power
  • Repetition is a feature the chorus loop is the point

Test: speak at tempo, then hum on one note — jaw locks = bad vowel, out of air = too long.

Sections & hook

Verse=particular (a name, a Tuesday). Chorus=universal, simplest, holds the title. Bridge=the turn. If one line survives, it's the chorus.

  • Hook types melodic (hum) · lyrical (title) · rhythmic (cadence)
  • Title placement first & last line of chorus
  • Repeat 3–5× per song — imprint, not annoy

Flow & rap craft

  • The bar 1 measure · "a 16" = 16-bar verse
  • Flow / pocket delivery rhythm locked to drums
  • Triplet flow 3/beat gallop · double-time 2× density
  • Multisyllabic "area / scarier / barrier"
  • Internal + slant modern default; assonance chains
  • Spill-over enjamb past the bar = momentum
  • Entendre two meanings; reward the re-listen

🤖 Prompt anatomy & meta-tags

Style skeleton: [genre] + [mood] + [key/mode] + [BPM + feel] + [instruments w/ timbre] + [production] + [vocal + delivery] + [era]

Structure tags (own line): [Intro] [Verse] [Pre-Chorus] [Chorus] [Post-Chorus] [Hook] [Bridge] [Build] [Drop] [Break] [Instrumental] [Guitar Solo] [Outro] · inline: (whispered) (belted) (ad-lib: yeah)

Anti-"AI-ism"

Break default AABB+4/4. Vary rhyme scheme & line length · specify mode + instrumentation · request delivery ("behind-the-beat," "half-time bridge") · give the exact hook & where it repeats · kill your own clichés · mode + BPM beat ten adjectives · re-roll without mercy.

Worked example — neo-soul ballad

neo-soul ballad, aching, D Dorian, 72 BPM behind-the-beat, warm Rhodes + muted upright bass + brushed drums, vinyl warmth, breathy female alto, lush harmonies, 70s lo-fi

[Verse]
Cold coffee on the windowsill, the radio's still on
[Chorus]
But you don't call, you don't call
I'm learning how the quiet sounds
concrete image, open-vowel "call/all," Dorian sadness, ad-lib echoes.

Worked example — epic build

epic cinematic trailer, ominous→triumphant, C Lydian over D-min pedal, 60→120 accel, low brass + taiko + choir + soaring strings, huge hall, belted female lead

[Build]
A spark beneath the ash
[Drop][Chorus]
RISE — and the dark comes undone
RISE — we are the breaking sun
Lydian wonder, spondee "RISE" on stabs, open belt vowels, accel = build.

Worked example — boom-bap rap

90s boom-bap, dusty & confident, E minor, 88 BPM in the pocket, chopped soul sample, upright-bass loop, vinyl drums, raspy baritone, conversational behind-the-beat flow

[Verse]
Pen hit the pad like a hammer to anvil (anvil)
Every bar's a chapter, a mantra, a handle (yeah)
[Hook]
That's the pocket, that's the pocket where I live
multisyllabic chains (anvil/mantra/handle), enjambment, ad-libs double the rhyme.

Worked example — bardcore folk

bardcore medieval folk, wistful, A Aeolian→Dorian, 96 BPM 6/8 lilt, lute + hurdy-gurdy + wooden flute + frame drum, stone-hall reverb, mixed-gender harmonies, no modern instruments

[Verse]
By candle-low the rivers run, the watchman calls the hour
[Chorus]
Oh carry me, oh carry me, where the green roads bend
6/8 anapestic gallop, open "oh/me" sustained vowels, refrain chorus, modal color.

📜 The 10 Commandments of Lyric Craft

Serve the breath — can't sing it in one breath at tempo? cut it.
Stress lands on the strong beat — never mis-stress a word to fit.
Open vowels on the money notes — save "ah/oh" for the peaks.
The chorus is simplest, widest, most-repeated — and holds the title.
Universal through the specific — name the diner, not "the pain."
Earn repetition — every loop chosen, no filler.
Rhyme is a tool, not a tyrant — break AABB; use slant, internal, multi.
Show senses, don't state feelings — cold coffee over "I'm sad."
Match mode & tempo to the emotion — Dorian + 72 BPM > ten adjectives.
Rewrite — first drafts are clay; the song is in the second pass.

🤖 Prompt corollary: tell the AI the mode, the BPM+feel, the timbre, the delivery, and the exact hook — then re-roll without mercy.

Harmony

Major=bright, minor=dark. Brightness Lyd·Ion·Mixo·Dor·Aeo·Phryg·Locr. Function T→PD→D→T (I→IV/ii→V→I). The 4 chords I–V–vi–IV; sad vi–IV–I–V. V→I=stop, IV→I=amen, →V=question, V→vi=fake-out. Borrow iv=saddest chord; Picardy 3rd=hope.

Rhythm

Backbeat=snare on 2 & 4. 4/4=default, 6/8=rolling, 3/4=waltz. Swing=long-short. BPM 60–80 slow · 90–115 groove · 120–130 dance · 140+ energy. Each chorus bigger; subtract before the hit.

Song form

Intro→V→PreC→Chorus→V→PreC→Chorus→Bridge→Final Chorus→Outro. Verse=specific, Chorus=universal+title+simplest, Bridge=the turn.

Meter

iamb da-DUM (talk) · trochee DUM-da (hook) · anapest da-da-DUM (gallop) · dactyl DUM-da-da (waltz) · spondee DUM-DUM (stabs). Ballad meter 8.6.8.6 ABCB = half of all songs.

Rhyme & lyric

Don't rhyme all AABB — use slant, internal, multisyllabic, assonance. Stressed syllable on the strong beat, always. Show senses, not feelings. Universal through the specific. Open vowels on big notes. Best line last.

Prompt skeleton

[genre]+[mood]+[key/mode]+[BPM+feel]+[instruments w/ timbre]+[production]+[vocal+delivery]+[era]. Mode + BPM beat ten adjectives. Re-roll without mercy.
